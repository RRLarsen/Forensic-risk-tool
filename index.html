Empirical research

Empirical studies in forensic risk assessment usually follow groups of offenders or patients over time, score them on structured tools at baseline, and then test how well those scores predict later violence or reoffending. Researchers typically use cohort or case–control designs, extract risk scores (e.g., HCR-20) from files or assessments, and examine predictive validity with statistics like AUCs, sensitivity, and specificity.
